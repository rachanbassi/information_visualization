Project Prototype

Dataset Summary:

The dataset that I have chosen for this project can be found in the UCI machine learning repository. It contains social, demographic and behavioural information about students at a Portugese school. The data also contains information about their mathematics grades. For this project, I wish to gain more insight into student performance based on social and demographic features. I also aim to extract any obvious patterns in the data that can be found to affect student performance. A full detailed description of the dataset can be found here. In terms of preprocessing, I grouped the number of absences (in days) into 5 bins: 6-10 days, 16-20 days, 11-15 days, >20 days and <5 days. The number of hours spent studying were also grouped into 4 bins: <2 hours, >10 hours, 2-5 hours and 5-10 hours. You will notice that bars representing absences for 11-15 days and <5 days are missing in the study time category of >10 hours. This is due to the fact that the average grade in these categories is zero. I am currently trying to come up with an ideal binning technique so that every bar is non-empty bars.

Sketch Summary:

The sketch that I have chosen for my prototype is a stacked bar chart which transitions into a grouped bar chart. The x axis represents the categories of the amount of time students spend studying. Within these categories, I have further grouped the data by the number of days the students are absent. The y axis represents the average grade for each of these categories. The graph is initially a stacked bar chart which transitions into a grouped bar chart. I have included an option of the user to select a grouped or stacked view by choosing the grouped and stacked options on the top right hand. The legend indicates the number of absent days a student has taken. The idea was to find any pattern in the number of absences and the amount of time students spend studying. Do student who take a lot of days off spend their absent days studying? Or perhaps they are studying more to make up for the days they took off from school? And how do different categories perform in terms of average grades. A detailed description of the the visualizations I plan to implement for this project can be found here.
